---
title: "Postman: Download Result Files"
---

## 1. Get your Download ID

After running your query with `"download": true`, you can grab your `download ID` from the response you get in Postman.

<Image src="https://files.readme.io/a7bc70c-8.jpg" width="1920" height="1080" alt="Postman response showing download ID after running a query" noCaption />

## 2. Open the "Get Download" Endpoint

Click "Get Download" in the "Downloads" folder to begin the download process.

<Image src="https://files.readme.io/ec23cab-2.jpg" width="1920" height="1080" alt="Get Download endpoint in the Downloads folder" noCaption />

## 3. Request Your Result File(s)

Replace `:id` with your `download ID` number you got from Step 1. In this example, the `download ID` was `40`.

<Image src="https://files.readme.io/bbd5f87-3.jpg" width="1920" height="1080" alt="Replacing :id with download ID number in the request" noCaption />

## 4. Downloading The Files

Copy the URL listed in the `"results"`.

<Image src="https://files.readme.io/323305c-4.jpg" width="1920" height="1080" alt="Response showing results URL to copy" noCaption />

<Callout kind="info">
**Result Files**

Result files are generated for every 100mb of records. Depending on the number of records downloading and your view, you may have many result file links, not just one.
</Callout>

Paste the results URL into your browser.

<Image src="https://files.readme.io/a092287-5.jpg" width="1920" height="1080" alt="Pasting results URL into browser address bar" noCaption />

Hit Enter to trigger the download. Then enjoy your dataset!

<Image src="https://files.readme.io/a6da573-6.jpg" width="1920" height="1080" alt="Download triggered in browser" noCaption />
